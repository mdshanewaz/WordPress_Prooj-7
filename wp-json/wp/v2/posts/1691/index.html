Coffee\u00a0is\u00a0so much more than a beverage \u2014 it\u2019s a community. By taking a look through some of the best coffee blogs out there, you\u2019ll get an introduction to just\u00a0how broad and diverse the world of coffee really is<\/a>.<\/p>\n

Maybe you\u2019re a novice whose infatuation is rapidly turning into something more serious. Or maybe you\u2019re a connoisseur with a thirst for knowledge on\u00a0brewing techniques<\/a>. A lot of these bloggers have been there, and they enthusiastically share tips, experiments, and comparisons that will have you making top-notch craft brews in no time.<\/p>\n

The social aspect of coffee drives some other blogs, which focus on coffee-forward global travel itineraries and coffee destinations, and introduce you to the people behind them. With coffee and community being inseparable, many of the best coffee blogs also touch on issues like sustainability and ethics. We all like a beverage we can really feel good about, and these blogs will help you get there.<\/p>\n

In this selection of some of the best coffee blogs we know (presented in no particular order), you\u2019re sure to find new things to love about coffee. Welcome to the conversation.<\/p>\n

1. Coffee Cantata<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

The catchphrase for Coffee Cantata<\/a>,\u00a0\u201cCoffee: Music in a Cup,\u201d captures creator Margaret\u2019s unpretentious approach to coffee connoisseurship. Her coffee reviews are cross-referenced by geographic origin and brew method. The precise details of the extraction process, like temperature and coffee-to-water ratio, are meticulously recorded so you can repeat the experience at home. Her tasting notes are vivid and delightful and they include serving thoughts, such as how the brew pairs with milk.<\/p>\n

Margaret \u2014 a professional musician \u2014 brings finesse to the art of tasting: \u201c\u2026 one sip of an Ethiopia Yirgacheffe and you will never mistake the flavor for a Sumatra, just like you would never mistake Mozart for Shostakovich.\u201d<\/p>\n

Her \u201coff-topic\u201d pieces also make excellent reading while you enjoy a carefully brewed, fresh cup of coffee.<\/p>\n

2. Sprudge<\/h2>\n

<\/h2>\n

Sprudge<\/a>\u00a0is widely recognized as one the best coffee blogs around. This is the place to find out about everything going on in the world of coffee.<\/p>\n

In Sprudge Wire, you can get up-to-date information on coffee-related news worldwide. For the traveler, coffee-forward city guides capture the rich essence of the distinct coffee cultures throughout the globe. There are also comprehensive listings for jobs in the best brands of specialty coffee.<\/p>\n

No time to read? They also have a podcast you can enjoy on the go with your sustainable travel mug in hand.<\/p>\n

3. The Coffee Compass<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

The Coffee Compass<\/a>\u00a0has a global outlook to navigating the world of craft coffee. You\u2019ll find interesting reviews of coffee shops from around the globe.<\/p>\n

One of the site\u2019s founders, Michael Butterworth, is a coffee consultant and educator, as well as a licensed Q Grader. He\u2019s based in Istanbul, while his founding partner, Darren Jennings \u2014 creator of the app RoastWatch \u2014 is in San Francisco.<\/p>\n

Professional equipment is highlighted in pieces like\u00a0How the Ikawa Coffee Roaster Changed Barista Competitions<\/a>. They also have insightful gear reviews and excellent instructional articles for getting the best performance out of your gear, like making iced coffee in an AeroPress. Book reviews and interviews introduce personalities from the world of coffee.<\/p>\n

4. Coffee Review<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

If you\u2019re interested in becoming a more informed coffee drinker,\u00a0Coffee Review<\/a>\u00a0is the best coffee blog to begin your journey.<\/p>\n

This blog, which has been running for over two decades, starts from the premise that coffee is as nuanced and complex a beverage as wine is, and deserves similarly clear and rigorous criteria for assessment. The aroma, acidity, body, flavor, and aftertaste of coffees are rated, and the fullness of the coffee experience unfolds.<\/p>\n

Coffee Review will help you to develop a more discerning palate, determine what factors affect the taste, and learn insider lingo, like Agtron numbers (which determine roast color). You\u2019ll also find excellent equipment reports, comparisons, and ratings.<\/p>\n

5. I Need Coffee<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

I Need Coffee<\/a>, started nearly 20 years ago by coffee enthusiast Michael Allen Smith, has stayed true to its mission: celebrating coffee and helping readers make the best possible coffee at home.<\/p>\n

Through tutorials on topics like using an upside-down AeroPress, making low-tech traditional coffees like Turkish coffee, or using a CoffeeSock, I Need Coffee helps readers maximize the home-coffee experience. You\u2019ll even find ambitious and fun projects, like roasting your own coffee beans in your home oven.<\/p>\n

You\u2019ll enjoy informative sections like\u00a0Latte Lingo<\/a>,\u00a0which includes fun notes on specialized coffee jargon.<\/p>\n

6. Coffee Stylish<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

Coffee Stylish<\/a>\u00a0creator Danijela is dedicated to helping the home coffee drinker brew seriously good coffee, offering tutorials on variety of accessible topics, such as how to make great coffee with what you already have and how to keep your coffee maker clean.<\/p>\n

If you\u2019re looking to upgrade your equipment, check out her insightful reviews. She also has a dedicated Keurig coffee maker resource page. Her enormously handy conversion chart ensures you\u2019ll always be able to brew the perfect cup of any size, for just one or for a crowd.<\/p>\n

There\u2019s also plenty of fanciful inspiration: If you like indulgent coffee drinks, you\u2019ll love her recipes for anything from a coconut milk mocha to a dulce de leche iced coffee float.<\/p>\n

7. The Coffeetographer<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

For webzine\u00a0The Coffeetographer<\/a>, the culture of independent and specialty coffee serves as a source of artistic inspiration. Sections on music, art, fashion, film, and travel all highlight the ways coffee is integral to a broad spectrum of cultural experience.<\/p>\n

The journal offers evocative, photo-illustrated vignettes, and thoughts. The coffeetography section combines photography and stories of people from coffee culture, creating a worldwide sense of community.<\/p>\n

8. Bean Ground<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

On\u00a0Bean Ground<\/a>, Mark Morphew \u2014 a passionate home-brewing enthusiast\u00a0with years of professional food and beverage experience \u2014 shares detailed, useful information geared to fellow home-brewers.<\/p>\n

Brew guides\u00a0include topics like maximizing your success with a Moka Pot or a Hario V60 brew guide for beginners. There\u2019s a whole section devoted to pour-over methods and gear, and another section for every type of home coffee maker with plenty of reviews.<\/p>\n

In the Coffee College section, Morphew shares his experiments and research, explains fundamentals like the\u00a0difference between Arabica and Robusta<\/a>,\u00a0and shares a rich backstory in\u00a0Everything You Ever Wanted To Know About Sumatra Coffee<\/a>.<\/p>\n

9. Jimseven<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

Jimseven<\/a>\u00a0is the\u00a0blog of James Hoffman, author of \u201cThe World Atlas of Coffee.\u201d He delivers detailed, in-depth information on diverse coffee topics. The industry-centered posts \u2014 like on quality vs. scale, or on the confusion of expensive coffee \u2014 make a nice read for both professionals or interested amateurs. James has a clear voice and an engaging style that makes for good reading.<\/p>\n

10. The Way to Coffee<\/h2>\n

<\/p>\n

On\u00a0The Way to Coffee<\/a>\u00a0blog, culture and coffee are intertwined. We join creator Resi on her quest to seek out authentic and unique coffee experiences around the globe.<\/p>\n

The result is a collection of coffee-forward city guides focusing on craft coffee and the people who make it. Visually, this is one of the best coffee blogs around \u2014 the stories really come to life through Resi\u2019s stellar photography. There\u2019s also an excellent section on specialty coffees and what she has learned from observing and engaging with professional baristas.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
